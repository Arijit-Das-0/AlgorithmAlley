Buy Maximum Stocks

Problem Statement

In a stock market, there is a product with its infinite stocks. The stock prices are given for N days, where price[i] denotes the price of the stock on the ith day. A customer can buy at most i stock on the ith day. Find the maximum number of stocks a customer can buy with an initial amount of k.

Example 1:

Input:
price = [10,7,19]
k = 45
Output: 
4
Explanation: 
A customer purchases 1 stock on day 1, 2 stocks on day 2, and 1 stock on day 3 for 10, 7 * 2 = 14 and 19 respectively.
Hence, the total amount is 10 + 14 + 19 = 43 and the number of stocks purchased is 4.

Example 2:

Input: 
price = [7,10,4]
k = 100
Output: 
6
Explanation:
Buy on all 3 days.

Code

                
int buyMaximumProducts(int n, int k, int price[]){
    vector> v;
    for (int i = 0; i < n; ++i)
        v.push_back({price[i], i + 1});

    sort(v.begin(), v.end());

    int ans = 0;

    for (auto it: v) {
        int maxBuy = min(k / it.first, it.second);
        ans += maxBuy;
        k -= it.first * maxBuy;
    }
    return ans;
}

Complexity

Time Complexity: O(NlogN)

Auxiliary Space: O(N)
